Weekly Meeting Format

Every meeting follows the same four-point agenda — simple, efficient, and focused on moving the project forward. Sessions should not exceed 60 minutes.

Standard Agenda — Every Meeting

1

What did you do this week?

Each researcher reports progress against last week's stated goal. Be specific — deliverables, not effort. Aim for two to three sentences per person.

2

What worked?

Highlight successful steps, useful databases, helpful papers, or insights gained. Share resources that others might benefit from. Positive results are worth recording.

3

What broke?

Surface blockers, errors, confusion, or dead ends early so the team can problem-solve together before they become delays. No blame — only solutions.

4

What is next week's goal?

Define one clear, concrete, deliverable-oriented goal for the coming week. It should be specific enough that success is unambiguous at the next meeting.

📖

Phase 1 Meeting Goal

All members can explain exosomes and circRNAs in plain language. Written summaries submitted. Live R code demonstrated during the meeting session.

🔍

Phase 2 Meeting Goal

Shared spreadsheets reviewed together. Inclusion/exclusion decisions for exosome filtering discussed and recorded with explicit reasoning.

🕸️

Phase 3–4 Meeting Goal

Live demo of analysis outputs: differential expression results, Cytoscape network screenshot, and initial Kaplan–Meier survival curves presented each week.

✍️

Phase 5 Meeting Goal

Draft figures and text sections reviewed. Each researcher reads and annotates at least one colleague's methods section before every Phase 5 meeting.
